# Why Walmart Pays Poverty Wages While Its Stock Makes Billionaires Richer Every Hour | YouTube Short Script
**Niche:** Scary Real Statistics Visualized
**Duration:** ~50 seconds
**Style:** Dramatic / Conspiracy-core

---

## SCRIPT

---

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:05]**
> *[Extreme close-up of a Walmart price tag. Numbers flicker. Screen cuts to black.]*

"A Walmart worker needs **3 jobs** to afford rent. The Waltons made **$4 million** richer while you read this."

---

**[BUILD — 0:05–0:20]**
> *[Slow aerial drone shot of a massive Walmart distribution center. Workers look like ants. Cut to a stock ticker climbing.]*

"The average Walmart associate earns **$14 an hour.** That's **$29,000 a year.** Below the poverty line for a family of four. Meanwhile, the six Walton heirs collectively hold over **$260 billion** in wealth. A fortune built entirely on the gap between what workers earn... and what the company keeps."

---

**[TWIST — 0:20–0:35]**
> *[Split screen: a worker scanning groceries on one side. A stock graph surging on the other. Text burns onto the screen: "WHO FUNDS THE DIVIDEND?"]*

"Here's what they don't say out loud. Walmart spends roughly **$8 billion a year** buying back its own stock — inflating share prices, making executives and heirs wealthier by the minute. That's **$8 billion** that could have doubled every worker's salary. Twice over. They chose the stock. Every single year."

---

**[OUTRO / CTA — 0:35–0:50]**
> *[Screen goes pitch black. A single cash register receipt unfurls slowly. The total at the bottom reads: $0.00 — labeled "YOUR SHARE."]*

"You shop there. You fund the machine. And the machine was never built for you."

"**The shelf doesn't stock itself. The dividend does.**"
