## SCRIPT

---

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:05]**
> *[Extreme close-up of a hand reaching into a chip bag in slow motion. Screen flickers to black. Single white text appears: "You were never in control."]*

"What if your cravings were never yours to begin with?"

---

**[BUILD — 0:05–0:20]**
> *[Cut to rapid-fire visuals: laboratory scientists, brain scans lighting up, corporate boardrooms. Dark red data overlays pulse across the screen.]*

"Food scientists are paid millions to find the **'bliss point'** — the exact combination of sugar, salt, and fat that **overrides your brain's stop signal.** Processed food triggers dopamine responses **nearly identical to cocaine.** The industry spends **over $14 billion per year** on food marketing alone — most of it targeting children."

---

**[TWIST — 0:20–0:35]**
> *[Slow zoom into a nutrition label. Letters begin glitching and rearranging. Cut to a black screen with a single statistic burning onto it.]*

"There are **over 60 names for sugar** hidden inside ingredient lists. Maltose. Dextrose. Evaporated cane juice. Names designed in boardrooms — not kitchens. The FDA knew. The studies existed. The products hit shelves anyway. **Ultra-processed food now makes up 73% of the American food supply.**"

---

**[OUTRO / CTA — 0:35–0:50]**
> *[Camera slowly pulls back to reveal a family eating dinner, laughing, completely unaware. The image desaturates to grey. A low, resonant tone hums beneath the silence.]*

"Every craving you've ever felt. Every time you said 'just one more.' That wasn't weakness. That was engineering."

"**They didn't make food. They made a product designed to make you never stop buying it.**"
