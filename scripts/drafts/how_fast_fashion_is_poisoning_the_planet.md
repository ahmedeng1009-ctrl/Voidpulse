## SCRIPT

---

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:05]**
> *[Extreme close-up of murky, dye-stained water flowing through a river. Slow motion. Text burns onto screen: "YOU WORE IT ONCE."]*

"Every second, the equivalent of one garbage truck of clothing is buried or burned."

---

**[BUILD — 0:05–0:20]**
> *[Cut to aerial footage of landfills overflowing with discarded clothes. Counter ticking upward on screen. Dark orchestral swell begins.]*

"The fashion industry produces **92 million tons of textile waste every single year.** It consumes more energy than aviation and shipping **combined.** And the water used to make one cotton t-shirt? **2,700 liters.** Enough for one person to drink for **two and a half years.**"

---

**[TWIST — 0:20–0:35]**
> *[Sudden cut to a child working in a textile factory, hands moving mechanically. Camera slowly pulls back to reveal rows of workers. The river from the opening reappears — now confirmed as a factory discharge drain. Text overlay: "20% OF GLOBAL WATER POLLUTION."]*

"The rivers running through Bangladesh, Cambodia, and Indonesia are now **chemically toxic** — poisoned by the dyes and synthetics that made your fifteen-dollar shirt possible. Those communities drink that water. Their children swim in it. Fast fashion didn't just take their labor. It took their future."

---

**[OUTRO / CTA — 0:35–0:50]**
> *[Screen fades to black. A single white t-shirt slowly dissolves into black liquid. The VoidPulse logo pulses once. Text: "FOLLOW FOR TRUTHS THEY DON'T WANT VISUALIZED."]*

"The brands know. The shareholders know. And now — so do you."

"**The most dangerous pollution isn't invisible. It's on sale for $14.99.**"
