# The Ocean Is Dying In Silence | YouTube Short Script
**Niche:** Scary Real Statistics Visualized
**Duration:** ~50 seconds
**Style:** Dramatic / Conspiracy-core

---

## SCRIPT

---

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:05]**
> *[Extreme close-up of dark ocean water. A single plastic bag drifts past the lens. Dead silence, then a deep bass pulse.]*

"The ocean isn't just polluted. It's being **buried alive.**"

---

**[BUILD — 0:05–0:20]**
> *[Black screen. White numbers rapidly counting upward. Cut to aerial footage of the Great Pacific Garbage Patch.]*

"Every single minute, **the equivalent of one garbage truck of plastic** is dumped into the ocean. That's **11 million metric tons every year.** By 2050, there will be **more plastic in the sea than fish** — by weight. Let that sink in."

---

**[TWIST — 0:20–0:35]**
> *[Slow-motion footage of deep ocean darkness. Red data visualization lines pulse across the screen like a heartbeat flatlining.]*

"And most of it? Invisible. **Microplastics have now been found in the deepest point on Earth** — the Mariana Trench, nearly **36,000 feet below the surface.** There is no pristine ocean left. **Not one square mile.** Scientists have detected plastic in fish tissue, in Arctic ice... and in **human blood.**"

---

**[OUTRO / CTA — 0:35–0:50]**
> *[The ocean surface filmed from below, light fractured and fading. Slow fade to black. A single stat burns onto screen.]*

"We didn't lose the ocean slowly. We buried it **in plain sight**, one disposable bottle at a time."

"**The ocean remembers every piece of plastic ever made. Because it still has all of it.**"

## STATS SOURCES
- UNEP (2021): 11 million metric tons of plastic enter oceans annually
- Ellen MacArthur Foundation (2016): More plastic than fish by weight by 2050
- Newcastle University / WWF (2019): Microplastics found in Mariana Trench
- Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam (2022): Microplastics detected in human blood
